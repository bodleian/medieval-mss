Physical Description

Secundo Folio: e cruciatibus
Form: codex
Support: Written on 195 membrane leaves, some with blemishes repaired before writing, preceded by a paper flyleaf (s. xviii) and a medieval membrane flyleaf and followed by a paper flyleaf (s. xviii).

Fore- and endleaves: fol. ir, number; fol. iv, blank; fol. iir, blank; fol. iiv, ex libris; fol. Irv, blank.

Extent: 195 leaves
Dimensions (leaf): 280 × 200 mm.

Collation

Collation: 1-248 25 three. Quire numbers up to xxi, with gaps. One fragmentary catchword remains, on fol. 96v.

Layout

Written in two columns, each 200-5 × 60 mm. 31 lines. Pricked in outer and inner margins, ruled in crayon, and folded HSOS.

Hand(s)

A good protogothic bookhand. Fols. 193-5, a separate quire, are in a bookhand of s. xiiiin which is virtually gothic in character although the only fused letters are de and pp. Like the rest of the text these leaves are written above top line. Punctuation is by point and punctus elevatus, but that of fols. 193-5 is by low and high point, punctus elevatus, and punctus interrogativus.

Decoration

Good two/five-line capitals in red-and-green, some plain and some with marginal extensions, some ornamented with a white pattern. Running numbers of books are red and in lists of capitula small capitals and figures are in alternate red and green.

Binding

Sewn on five bands between heavy, projecting, square-edged wooden boards covered with white alum-tawed skin (s. xviii). At the front is a flyleaf (fol. ii), a former pastedown, apparently from the back of the volume since it has five (formerly six) holes from the chain-plate at the bottom (position 1), and at top left, in the margin, is a cutout for the clasp at the top, which shows that leaf is back-to-front. The last leaf of the text (fol. 195) has been next to this flyleaf; at the bottom it shows six stains from nails of the chain-plate. At the bottom of fols. 192v and 193rv are small rust marks. On the spine is a black leather lozenge-shaped label with ‘9’ on it and a black leather label with a title. Edges are yellowed and there is a fore-edge title.

History

Origin: s. xiiex ; England

Provenance and Acquisition

At the bottom of fol. 1r it is ex libris style 1, ‘Liber Collegij animarum omnium fidelium Oxonie ex dono Regis Henrici vjti. Oretis pro Inclito statu eiusdem.’ (textura, s. xv). Ker, All Souls, List 1, no. 20, with other books in Henry yes gift. At the top of the page is ex libris style 2, ‘Liber Collegii Omnium Animarum Fidelium Defunctorum de Oxon.’ (also on fol. iiv in textura perhaps of s. xv, reading ‘...in Oxon...’). No. 1408 (7) in CMA.

All Souls library identifications, on which see Introduction, are, on the pastedown, ‘vv.9.12’ and ‘ww:5:9’ (both deleted), ‘M.S. 46’ (figure added), ‘138’ (pencil), ‘L.R. 2.c.10’ (pencil), and bookplate no. 10, and on fol. ir ‘7.2 (pencil)’; on fol. 1r are ‘K.s.d’ and ‘P.<..>’. There are marginalia in a secretary hand of c.1600 and occasionally (e.g. fols. 149r, 150r) in a humanist hand of the same date: all may, indeed, be by the same writer.
