All Souls College MS. 23

G. Savonarola, Exposition upon Psalm 51; etc.

Contents

1.
Rubric: Fol. 1v An exposition after the maner of a contemplacyon vpon the lj. psalme called <Miserere mei deus written over an erasure, s. xvii(?)>.
Incipit: Fol. 2r Alas wretche that I am conforteles | forsaken of all men, which haue offended both heuen and earth
Explicit: Fol. 65r thou hast prepared for them that loue the. So be yt.
Savonarola, Girolamo, 1452–1498,
Girolamo Savonarola (Hieronymus of Ferrara), Exposition upon Psalm 57;
Stegmuller, Bibl. 3482.
A translation from Fratris Hieronymi Ferrariensis expositiones in Psalmos (Venice, 1505).
For many printed editions, see STC 21789.3-21794.
On the text see H. C. White, The Tudor Books of Private Devotion (Madison, 1951), 79—80. IMEP 1.
Language(s): English with Latin
2. Fols. 65v-77r.
Prayers and litany in English, tempore Edward VI.

The litany is that issued in 1544 and printed as part of the Book of Common Prayer. IMEP as above.

Language(s): English

Physical Description

Secundo Folio: onely art.
Form: codex
Support: Written on 80 leaves of rather stiff membrane preceded by two post-medieval paper flyleaves and two membrane leaves and followed by one membrane and two post-medieval flyleaves.

Fore- and endleaves: fol. ir, blank; fol. iv, numbers; fol. iirv, blank; fol. iiir, numbers and title; fols. iiiv-ivr, blank; fol. ivv, number; fol. 1r, number, pen-trials; fol 1v, title,Luttrell monogram; fols. Ir-IIIv, blank.

On fol. 78rv a later writer copied out fols. 1r-1v/3 of the main text. Fol. 77v is blank. Fols. 79r—80v are blank except for a pentrial on fol. 80v.

Extent: 80 leaves
Dimensions (leaf): 82 × 63 mm.
Foliation: Post-medieval quire numbers in pencil on the first leaf of quires in the lower margin well into the gutter.

Collation

1-136 142. No catchwords.

Layout

Written in one column, 67 × 45 mm. 16-24 lines. No prickings visible. Frame-ruled in crayon and folded HSOS(?).

Hand(s)

Secretary. Punctuation by low point, comma, and virgula.

Item 2 is in secretary script, except fol. 78rv, in italic. In several hands.

Binding

Sewn on three bands between millboards covered with blind-stamped and -ruled 18th-century calf with the small All Souls coat of arms on both covers.

History

Origin: s. xv1, xvimed ; England

Provenance and Acquisition

On fol. 1v is Nathaniel Luttrell's stamped monogram and ‘1693’ added by hand. On fol. iiir is ‘51 Psalme After ye manner of Contemplation Paraphrased’, perhaps in his hand. On the College's acquisition of his manuscripts see under MS 17. All Souls library identifications, on which see Introduction, are, on fol. iv, ‘v.v.13.1’ (deleted), ‘MS. 23’, and ‘L.R. 1.f.5’ (pencil); on fol. iiir, ‘19.10’ (pencil); on fol. ivv, ‘104’ (pencil).
