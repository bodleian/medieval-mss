All Souls College MS. 44

Statuta, etc. in Latin and French

Physical Description

Secundo Folio: A quod usura; B de feodo; C W qu ci est; D Regis. Si vna
Form: codex
Support: Written on 176 membrane leaves of average quality preceded by a paper leaf (s. xviii) and five medieval membrane flyleaves and followed by two paper flyleaves (s. xviii).

Fore- and endleaves: fols. ir, number; fol. iv, blank; fols. iir-iiircapitula; fols. iiiv-vvblank; fol. vir, title; fol. viv, monogram; fols. Ir-IIv, blank.

Extent: 176 leaves
Dimensions (leaf): 115 × 65 mm.
Foliation: Leaves are numbered on versos in small roman figures, s. xv, and in modern pencil on rectos.

Collation

Collation: 18 | 2-912 | 10-1312 142 || 1510 1612. In A-B quires 1, 2-7 are signed in capital roman letters and lower-case figures to the middle of the quire. Traces of catchwords remain. In C there are no quire numbers or signatures but traces of catchwords remain. In D there are neither quire numbers or signatures nor catchwords.

Binding

Sewn on four bands in 18th-century calf with a gold-tooled spine.

History

Origin: s. xivmed, s. xv ; England

Provenance and Acquisition

Fol. 176v ‘Liber Johan Coniers pharma-copolae’ (s. xvii2); for materials relating to this London apothecary now with the Sloane Manuscripts in the British Library see E. J. L. Scott, Index to the Sloane Manuscripts in the British Museum (London, 1904, repr. 1971), 121. On fol. viv is the stamped monogram of Narcissus Luttrell, the impression of which can be seen on the preceding leaves as far as fol. iii, with ‘1693’ written beside it. On fol. vir he wrote ‘Magna Carta. &c. Latin’. On the College's acquisition of Luttrell's manuscripts see under MS 17 above.

All Souls library identifications, on which see Introduction, are, on the front pastedown, ‘v.v.13.2’ (deleted), ‘M.S. 44’, ‘133’ (pencil), ‘L.R. 1.f.4’ (pencil), and bookplate no. 10; on fol. ir, ‘19..9’.

All Souls College MS. 44, part C

Contents

Language(s): Anglo-Norman

1.
Incipit: Fol. 105r. Counte de drete. Ceo vous monstre. W. de .M. qe ci est qe J". de .O. qe la est atort luy deforce [de] vn mees etc. oue les apurtenances in .N. Et pur ces atort
Explicit: Fol. 154v. Il fut destreint et de luy leue atort et a ces damages etc. Que' de champartours &c. Explicit: expliceat: et cetera.
A collection of 235 countes or narrationes, in Anglo-Norman.

S. xivmed, illustrating the forms for presenting typical cases of various kinds in court. Such collections were first printed in London(?), before 1501(?) (STC 18361) or London, 1504(?) (STC 18362). Many of those in our manuscript are printed in Novae narrationes, ed. E. Shanks and S. F. C. Milsom (Selden Society 80; London, 1963), where thirty-eight manuscripts (not including ours) are recorded. In our manuscript initials are used for the names of parties. The concluding phrase of the last item does not appear in Shanks and Milsom (B253) and seems to belong elsewhere.
