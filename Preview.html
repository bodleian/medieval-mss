All Souls College MS. 3

Biblia, etc.

Physical Description

Secundo Folio: (text) testantur
Form: codex
Support: Written on 334 membrane leaves of average quality except the first ten which are ofless good quality. Fols. i and v are raised pastedowns; ii-iv, I-IV are post-medieval paper.

Fore- and endleaves: fol. irv, text, = *A; fol. iir, numbers; fols. iiv-ivv, blank; fols. I—IV, blank; fol. V, text, = *A.

Extent: 334 leaves
Dimensions (leaf): 215 × 170 mm.

Collation

Collation: 14 26 || 310 412 5-3110 32-338 3412 354. No quire numbers, quire signatures, or catchwords remain.

Binding

Sewn on four bands and rebound (with some loss of marginal writing) between millboards covered with blind-ruled and -stamped leather (s. xvi/xvii). Endbands are blue and white. In the centre of each cover is a device surmounted by a letter F for ‘Freman’ (see History below): for an illustration of it see Oldham, EBSB, pl. Lxi, no. 1088, and his comment, p. 37. On the fore-edge is ‘Bib.’ Fols. i and v are raised pastedowns, *A above.

History

Origin: s. xiii1 ; England or France

Provenance and Acquisition

At the top of fol. 185r is an erased inscription in a hand of s. xvi, not recoverable by ultraviolet light. Inside the front cover, almost covered by an All Souls bookplate, is the bookplate of ‘Ralph Freman of Hammells in Corn' Hertford Esci.’, i.e. Dr Ralph Freman, prebendary of Salisbury and former fellow, who bequeathed a large collection of books to the College in 1744: see Craster, History, 91. On fol. iir is ‘A 5’, altered to ‘B 5’, pressmarks which correspond to two catalogues of the Freman library now in All Souls, one of 1713 (MS 426), p. 44, reversed, and one of before 1724 (MS 425), p. 113.

All Souls library identifications, on which see Introduction, are, on the Freman bookplate, ‘vv.5.2’ (deleted), ‘90’ (pencil, deleted), ‘MS.3’, and bookplate no. 10, on which is ‘L.R. 1.g.5’ (pencil); inside the upper board is ‘ww:7:4’ (deleted), on fol. iir is ‘18,1’ (pencil).

Physical Description

Form: codex

Layout

C(i) in two columns, each c.170 × 55-60 mm. 52-4 lines; C(ii) in three columns, each 205 × 45 mm. 54 lines; C(iii) in three columns, 205 × 45-50 mm. 61-2 lines. The main text, C(i), is pricked in inner and outer margins, ruled in pencil and folded FSOS.

Hand(s)

The main text, C(i), is in a compressed and heavily abbreviated yet competent small gothic bookhand.

C(ii) and C(iii) are by the same hand, the first in a small gothic bookhand and the second in a notularis.

Decoration

C(i), red-and-blue lombards of various large sizes at the beginnings of books, and in C(ii) each entry begins with a red capital. Book titles in red. Book numbers in red-and-blue with red or blue paraphs. Running titles in red-and-blue. Single-line red or blue initials in text to mark beginning of chapters. Rubrics in C(i) and C(ii).
